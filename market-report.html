Executive Summary
A precinct gaining momentum

Grey Lynn's office market has entered a compelling phase of growth, underpinned by a distinctive combination of heritage character, creative-sector demand, and proximity to Auckland's CBD fringe. Over the past 12 months, the precinct has recorded its strongest leasing activity since 2019, with average office rents advancing 6.8% year-on-year to $312 per square metre.

The area's appeal to design, technology, and professional services firms continues to strengthen. Tenant demand is being driven by businesses seeking alternatives to traditional CBD towers — favouring the walkability, village atmosphere, and character buildings that define Grey Lynn's commercial landscape.

For property owners positioned in this market, the outlook is favourable. Vacancy rates have tightened to 4.1% — well below the wider Auckland CBD fringe average of 7.8%. Properties that invest in modernisation and seismic compliance are commanding significant premiums, with Grade A refurbished offices achieving up to $380 per square metre.

Rental Trajectory

Grey Lynn office rents have grown consistently over the past three years, from $262/sqm in 2023 to $312/sqm in 2026. This 19% cumulative growth significantly outperforms the Auckland CBD fringe average of 8% over the same period. Character refurbished buildings are driving the premium end of the market.

🏠

Tenant Demand Profile

The precinct attracts a distinctive tenant mix: 38% creative and design firms, 24% professional services, 18% technology companies, and 20% other. Businesses are drawn to the walkable village environment, proximity to Ponsonby Road amenities, and the authentic character that heritage buildings provide.

💰

Investment Yields

Commercial property yields in Grey Lynn have compressed from 6.2% to 5.4% over 24 months, reflecting strong investor confidence. Well-leased character offices are transacting at cap rates of 5.0-5.5%, comparable to premium CBD fringe assets. Owner-occupier demand remains a key driver of capital values.

🚀

Supply Pipeline

Limited new supply is a defining feature of the Grey Lynn market. The heritage character of the area constrains large-scale development, creating natural scarcity. Only 2 new office developments (combined 1,200 sqm) are consented for delivery in 2027, representing less than 3% of existing stock.

Looking ahead: 2026 and beyond
