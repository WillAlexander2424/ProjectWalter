Section 02 · Walter Advisory

Clause-by-clause review

Each material clause assessed against 14,200+ comparable NZ commercial agreements and contemporary case law. Where Walter has a recommendation, it's clearly marked.

Alert
Clause 8.7 — Vendor warranty exclusions are unusually broad

The vendor has carved out warranties for any defect "discoverable upon reasonable inspection". Under NZ contract law (Property Law Act 2007 s.225), this is enforceable but the breadth here is well above market practice. In comparable BTR transactions reviewed, vendors typically warrant against latent defects for 12-24 months post-settlement.

Recommend Negotiate a 12-month latent defect warranty with $500k cap
Review
Clause 11.2 — Settlement extension mechanism is one-sided

Only the vendor has the right to extend settlement (up to 20 working days) if CCC is delayed. The purchaser has no equivalent extension right for finance or OIA delays. This is asymmetric and not consistent with current REINZ best practice for BTR transactions.

Recommend Mutual extension right of 15 working days for finance/OIA
Review
Clause 14 — Default interest rate at 14% is high

Default interest is set at 14% per annum on overdue balances. Current OCR is 4.25% — default rates of 12-18% are normal market range, but most comparable agreements set this at OCR + 5% (currently ~9.25%) which is more defensible if challenged as a penalty.

Recommend Cap at OCR + 5% or 11% — whichever is lower
Alert
Schedule 3 — Tenancy schedule incomplete (12 of 47 units listed)

Only 12 tenancies are detailed in Schedule 3. The remaining 35 units are described as "to be tenanted prior to settlement". Without confirmed tenancies, the rental yield calculation cannot be verified, and the purchaser is exposed to vacancy risk on ~74% of the asset.

Recommend Add a vendor warranty of 90% occupancy at settlement, with $/unit price reduction for shortfall
Good
Clause 5 — Due diligence period of 15 working days is generous

Standard market practice for BTR transactions of this scale is 10-15 working days. The 15 working days here gives Whanau Ora adequate time for full physical inspection and financial review.

Good
Clause 19 — OIA condition is standard

The Overseas Investment Act condition is drafted in line with the standard REINZ template. No amendments needed.
